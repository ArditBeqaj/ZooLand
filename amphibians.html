Red Eye Frog

The Red-Eyed Tree Frog is a small amphibian with striking red eyes and a bright green body. Found in Central American rainforests, it uses sticky toe pads to climb trees. These nocturnal frogs eat insects and use their vivid colors to startle predators and avoid being eaten.Their blue and yellow side stripes add to their distinctive appearance, making them a unique and fascinating species in their ecosystem.

Axolotl

The axolotl is a unique amphibian native to Mexico, known for its ability to regenerate limbs and other body parts. Unlike most amphibians, axolotls remain in their larval stage throughout their lives, a condition called neoteny. They have feathery external gills that provide a distinctive appearance and allow them to breathe underwater.

Catch the Frog

The frog is a member of a diverse and largely carnivorous group of short-bodied, tailless amphibians composing the order Anura. Frogs are known for their jumping abilities, croaking sounds, bulging eyes, and slimy skin. They are found in a variety of habitats, ranging from tropical forests to ponds and marshes.
