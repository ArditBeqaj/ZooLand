Mammals

Lion

The lion (Panthera leo) is a large carnivorous feline known for its majestic appearance and social behavior. Native to Africa and a small population in India, lions live in groups called prides. Males are distinguished by their prominent manes, which vary in color from blond to black. Lions are apex predators and play a crucial role in their ecosystems.

Elephant

The elephant is the largest land animal, with males reaching up to 13 feet in height and weighing up to 14,000 pounds. They are known for their long trunks, large ears, and tusks, which are actually elongated incisor teeth. Elephants are herbivores and play a crucial role in shaping their ecosystems.

Click the Cheetah

The cheetah (Acinonyx jubatus) is a large cat known for its incredible speed, capable of reaching 60 to 70 miles per hour in short bursts covering distances up to 500 meters, and has the ability to accelerate from 0 to 60 miles per hour in just a few seconds. Native to Africa and central Iran, the cheetah's slender, long-legged body is built for speed. It is an apex predator in its environment.

Zebra

Zebras are known for their distinctive black-and-white stripes, which serve as a form of camouflage and protection against predators. They are social animals that live in herds and are found primarily in the savannas and grasslands of Africa. Zebras typically stand about 4.5 to 5 feet tall at the shoulder and can weigh between 770 to 990 pounds.

Giraffe

Giraffes are the tallest land animals, reaching up to 18 feet in height. They inhabit African savannas and woodlands, feeding mainly on leaves from acacia trees. Their long necks and unique coat patterns are distinctive features. Giraffes play a key role in seed dispersal and are vital to their ecosystems.
